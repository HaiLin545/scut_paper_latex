\chapter[摘要]{摘\quad 要} 
\setlength{\parindent}{2em}

随着元宇宙、虚拟现实及数字娱乐等应用场景的持续发展，构建高保真、可驱动且具备结构化语义的穿衣数字化身，已成为计算机视觉与计算机图形学领域的重要研究课题。围绕该目标，现有研究通常需要同时解决两个关键问题，它们直接决定了数字化身的真实感和可用性：其一，实现人体在跨姿态条件下的稳定三维重建；其二，生成与穿着者体型相匹配的服装结构。现有方法在上述两个方面仍存在不足。在人体重建方面，基于三维高斯溅射的方法普遍存在高斯几何属性与底层网格拓扑关联不足的问题，模型在分布外姿态下容易出现表面破碎与几何伪影。在服装生成方面，参数化方法普遍缺乏人体尺度的显式参照，易产生版型尺寸失配，并强依赖大规模训练数据。针对上述问题，本文以“几何先验”为主线，开展以下研究工作：

针对现有三维高斯溅射人体重建方法在新姿态下的几何失真问题，本文提出了一种基于拓扑绑定的三维高斯人体重建方法。该方法利用可变形网格的拓扑结构，设计了面片高斯与顶点高斯两类互补基元，分别以三角面片内切圆和网格顶点为锚点，将几何属性由网格拓扑解析推导获得，从而有效缓解高斯基元与网格表面的几何不一致问题，同时引入等边几何正则化以维护网格质量。在PeopleSnapshot与X-Avatar数据集上的实验结果表明，该方法在法向误差指标上与最优基线相比分别降低17.9\%和13.8\%；在X-Avatar数据集上，倒角距离降低15.9\%，同时模型大小降低44.5\%。

针对现有服装参数生成方法中人体尺寸约束不足且依赖大规模训练数据的问题，本文提出了一种基于人体测量先验的参数化服装生成方法。该方法首先将量体工具适配至SMPL拓扑，从单张图像提取人体测量值作为显式先验；继而通过信息分流策略将参数格式压缩为紧凑表示，将Token消耗降低90\%以上；最后通过几何锚点归一化与分步推理工作流，引导GPT-4o以零样本方式完成多模态参数推理。在CLoSe与4D-DRESS数据集上的实验表明，该方法分别取得1.621 cm和1.834 cm的倒角距离，较最优基线降低19.5\%和6.2\%，推理速度提升约4倍，且无需训练数据，结构合法率达100\%。

综上，本文通过在不同层级引入人体几何先验，分别缓解了数字化身动态外观渲染中的几何失真问题与参数化服装生成中的体型失配问题，为构建高质量、可交互的穿衣数字化身提供了有效的技术支撑。

\chapter[Abstract]{Abstract}
% \setlength{\parindent}{0em} % 英文摘要通常首行不缩进，段落间空行；若学校有格式要求可改为 2em

As application scenarios such as the Metaverse, virtual reality, and digital entertainment continue to expand, building clothed digital avatars that are high-fidelity, drivable, and semantically structured has become an important research problem in computer vision and computer graphics. Existing pipelines typically need to solve two coupled tasks: stable 3D human reconstruction across poses, and garment-parameter generation that matches the wearer's body shape. Current methods remain limited in both aspects. For human reconstruction, many 3D Gaussian Splatting-based methods do not tightly couple Gaussian geometry with the underlying mesh topology, which often leads to surface breakage and artifacts under out-of-distribution poses. For garment generation, parametric methods often lack explicit body-scale references, leading to size mismatch and strong dependence on large training sets. To address these issues, this thesis develops two methods under a unified geometric-prior framework.

For geometric distortion in pose-driven 3D Gaussian human reconstruction, this thesis proposes a topology-bound 3D Gaussian reconstruction method. The method introduces two complementary primitives---face Gaussians and vertex Gaussians---anchored at triangle incircles and mesh vertices, and derives their geometric attributes analytically from mesh topology. This design mitigates geometric inconsistency between Gaussian primitives and the mesh surface. An equilateral geometric regularization term is further introduced to maintain mesh quality. On PeopleSnapshot and X-Avatar, the proposed method reduces normal error by 17.9\% and 13.8\%, respectively, compared with the strongest baseline. On X-Avatar, it also reduces Chamfer distance by 15.9\% and shrinks model size by 44.5\%.

For parametric garment generation, this thesis proposes a body-measurement-prior-guided method to address insufficient body-size constraints and heavy training dependence in existing approaches. The method first adapts a measurement tool to SMPL topology and extracts body measurements from a single image as explicit priors. It then compresses the parameter format through an information-routing strategy, reducing token consumption by more than 90\%. Finally, it guides GPT-4o to perform zero-shot multimodal parameter reasoning through geometric-anchor normalization and a stepwise inference workflow. On CLoSe and 4D-DRESS, the method achieves Chamfer distances of 1.621\,cm and 1.834\,cm, improving over the best baselines by 19.5\% and 6.2\%, respectively. It also delivers about 4$\times$ faster inference, requires no training data, and reaches a 100\% structural validity rate.

In summary, by introducing geometric priors at different levels, this thesis mitigates geometric distortion in dynamic human appearance reconstruction and body-shape mismatch in parametric garment generation, providing practical technical support for high-quality and interactive clothed digital avatars.

\noindent \textbf{Keywords:} Digital Avatar Reconstruction; 3D Gaussian Splatting; Parametric Garment Generation; Geometric Priors
